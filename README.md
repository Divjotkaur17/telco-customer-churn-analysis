Project Overview

Customer churn is one of the most critical metrics for any subscription-based business. The objective of this project is to analyze customer behavior in the telecommunications sector to identify the primary drivers of customer attrition. By uncovering these patterns, businesses can implement targeted retention strategies.

Tools & Technologies Used:-

Microsoft Excel: Initial data exploration, sanity checks, and data cleaning (handling null/blank values in billing columns).
Power BI: Data modeling, DAX measure creation, and interactive dashboard design.

Key Metrics & KPIs:-

Total Customers: 7,043
Retained Customers: 5,174
Churned Customers: 1,869
Overall Churn Rate: 26.54%

Crucial Business Insights:-

Contract Type is the Biggest Driver: Customers on Month-to-Month contracts churn at a drastically higher rate (~42.7%) compared to those on 1-year or 2-year commitments.

The Impact of Tech Support: There is a strong negative correlation between Tech Support and churn. Customers lacking Tech Support leave at a much higher rate (~41.6%) than those who have it.

Fiber Optic Vulnerability: Counterintuitively, customers using Fiber Optic internet service have the highest churn rate (~41.8%) among internet types, indicating a potential issue with service reliability, pricing, or competitor offerings in that tier.

Technical Steps Taken
Cleaned raw CSV data to ensure correct data typing (converting text to currency/decimals).

Engineered custom DAX measures for dynamic calculations (e.g., CALCULATE, COUNTROWS, and DIVIDE for exact percentage tracking).

Designed a custom UI/UX background to structure the visual hierarchy and improve readability for non-technical stakeholders.
